================================================================================
RECOMMENDATION RATIONALE
================================================================================

Direction B is recommended because it:

1. MAXIMIZES ICP TRUST (Phase 1 research)
   - Blue = regulatory authority (highest trust signal)
   - Green = certification/approval (addresses compliance driver)
   - Amber = caution without aggression (safety-conscious)

2. OPTIMIZES MARKET POSITIONING
   - Blue keeps within comfort zone (80% of market uses it - safe)
   - Green differentiates (few competitors use it - novel but credible)
   - Perfect balance: Traditional enough to be credible + different enough to stand out

3. ENSURES ACCESSIBILITY
   - Best contrast ratios (all colors meet WCAG AA/AAA standards)
   - Best for color-blind safety (blue-green distinguishable by luminance)
   - Safest for older ICP audience (45-60 age group)

4. PROVIDES OPERATIONAL FLEXIBILITY
   - Blue is primary; easy to dial back if needed
   - Green is additive (cert sections, buttons); not forced throughout
   - Status colors are standard and unambiguous
   - Works across all content types

================================================================================
ACCESSIBILITY COMPLIANCE
================================================================================

All directions include:
- Full WCAG contrast ratio analysis
- Color-blindness safety review (deuteranopia/red-green blindness)
- Readability testing on off-white background (#FDFDFD)

Best Performer: Direction B (all colors WCAG AA/AAA compliant)
Second Best: Direction C (deep tones provide excellent contrast)
Concerns: Directions A & D have some colors at AA-only threshold

RECOMMENDATION: Pair all status colors with icons/text labels (liability protection).
Never use color alone for critical information.
